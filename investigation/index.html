In Oakville, geotechnical investigation addresses the shale and till deposits of the Halton Till Plain, where variable overburden and shallow bedrock demand precise stratigraphic profiling. Our work follows the Ontario Building Code and Canadian Foundation Engineering Manual to define bearing capacity, settlement risk, and groundwater conditions. On-site methods such as the CPT (Cone Penetration Test) deliver continuous data in low-access areas, while borehole drilling captures samples for lab-index testing, ensuring layered soil behaviour is fully characterized before design.

Residential subdivisions, mid-rise mixed-use builds, and infrastructure upgrades near creeks or the Lake Ontario shoreline routinely trigger these investigations. Pairing CPT with geophysical surveys helps map fill zones, buried valleys, or karst features that discrete borings alone could miss. The result is a defensible ground model that supports shoring, foundation selection, and stormwater management, keeping Oakville projects on schedule and compliant with municipal review requirements.

Geotechnical investigation in Oakville is the essential first step for any construction project, providing a detailed understanding of the subsurface conditions that will directly influence foundation design and long-term structural performance. The local geology, shaped by glacial Lake Iroquois, is characterized by the Halton Till—a complex sequence of silty clay to clayey silt till with variable sand and gravel lenses—overlying fractured Queenston Formation shale. A comprehensive investigation, guided by the Ontario Building Code (OBC) and Canadian Foundation Engineering Manual (CFEM), cuts through this variability to identify potential hazards such as soft, compressible soils, high groundwater tables common near the Lake Ontario shoreline, and the depth to competent bearing strata. This critical data is the foundation for our targeted Investigation services and specialized CPT (Cone Penetration Test) programs, ensuring your project is built on certainty, not assumptions.

Our investigative methodology in Oakville strictly adheres to the Canadian Standards Association (CSA) and ASTM International standards, employing a multi-faceted approach to accurately characterize the challenging Halton Till. This begins with a robust field program combining direct methods like hollow-stem auger drilling and Standard Penetration Testing (SPT) with advanced continuous profiling via seismic cone penetration testing (SCPTu). The SCPTu is particularly effective here for delineating thin drainage layers within the till and assessing soil behaviour type. These field techniques are complemented by our accredited Laboratory analysis, where index property tests are paramount. We perform Grain size analysis (sieve + hydrometer) to precisely classify the silty sand and clay fractions, a critical factor in frost susceptibility and drainage design, and Atterberg limits testing to determine the plasticity characteristics of the cohesive till matrix, which governs its shrink-swell potential and long-term strength.

Oakville's diverse development landscape, from luxury residential subdivisions on the Glen Abbey escarpment to mid-rise commercial structures and infrastructure renewal in established neighborhoods like Bronte, presents unique geotechnical challenges that demand a nuanced investigation. For a deep foundation project, such as a high-rise condominium near the lake, we integrate CPT data with laboratory strength testing on Shelby tube samples to model shaft friction and end-bearing capacity for driven piles or drilled caissons socketed into the Queenston Shale. In contrast, for a large industrial building on a greenfield site, our Field density test (sand cone method) is a standard quality control tool during construction to verify engineered fill compaction against the specified percentage of Standard Proctor maximum dry density. Every investigation is tailored, moving from a desktop study and preliminary site visit to a definitive, phased field and laboratory program that targets specific project risks.

The investigative process delivers a clear, actionable geotechnical report that serves as the project's subsurface blueprint. Following data synthesis from our field and In-Situ suites, we develop a precise ground model and provide definitive engineering recommendations. These deliverables include allowable bearing pressures for shallow foundations, lateral earth pressures for retaining wall design, seismic site classification per OBC Table 4.1.8.4.A, and detailed assessments of groundwater control and excavation stability. The ultimate value is risk mitigation and cost certainty: by defining the ground conditions accurately upfront, we empower structural engineers and contractors to optimize Foundations and earthworks, preventing costly change orders and construction delays. This seamless integration of local geological expertise, rigorous standardized testing, and practical engineering judgment ensures your Oakville project proceeds from investigation
